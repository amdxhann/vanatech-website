About Product

The aquaculture industry in Indonesia demonstrates a robust annual growth of up to 15%, specifically within the realm of shrimp cultivation. This highlights the need for better ways of doing things and the imperative for enhanced processing practices, especially in feed administration and water quality monitoring. Traditional challenges, such as uneven feed distribution and unmonitored water quality, have caused the rate of shrimp to be less than optimal and delayed harvest. To solve these issues, VANATECH introduces automated feed distribution and remote water quality control, powered by solar energy and utilizing brushless motor technology. Equipped with pH, temperature, and salinity sensors, the system monitors pool water quality and, through the Internet of Things (IoT), enables real-time monitoring via a website. Evaluation of this equipment shows a significant impact on shrimp productivity, exceeding conventional methods. The exhibits superior results, attributing it to uniform feed distribution and enhanced water quality. This improved growth is evident in the accelerated harvest schedule, which was reduced from 4 to 3 months, and a remarkable increase in harvested biomass from 1749 kg to 2799 kg. VANATECH's positive impact extends to the economic sector, vannamei shrimp farmers have experienced a 20-fold increase in income compared to conventional techniques. Monitoring the water quality in the system contributes to making the right decisions to maintain optimal conditions. In conclusion, VANATECH emerges as a transformative solution, fostering an ideal environment for shrimp growth and increasing the economic viability of aquaculture in Indonesia.
